In the present paper the author updates the taxonomie and floristic knowledge of foliicolous lichenized fungi in Argentina, with emphasis on Corrientes province, and adjacent Paraguay and southern Brazil. Fifteen families, 25 genera and 80 species were collected mainly in riparian forests. The following taxa are reported for the first time from Argentina: Actinopiaca strigulacea , Byssoloma absconditum , B. fade ni i , B. vanderystii , Calenia monospora , Fellhanera fuscatula , F. semecarpi , Gyalectidium caucasicum , G. denticulatum , Porina atropunctata , P. distans , Stridula orbicularis , and Trichothelium argenteum. Some taxa are briefly discussed and illustrated, with comments on their distribution. The relationships between lichens, algae and bryophytes which inhabit the phyllosphere in NE Argentina are also examined. Introduction The information on foliicolous lichens in Argentina is very scarce. Grassi (1950), although including 950 species in his Contribución al Catálogo de Liqúenes Argentinos , did not mention any foliicolous taxa. Generally, the knowledge on foliicolous lichens in southern South America is rather poor, with some isolated data about southern Brazil, Paraguay, and Uruguay in the monograph of Santesson (1952) and the papers of Schell &amp; Winkler (1981), Sérusiaux &amp; De Sloover (1986), and Osorio (1992). Santesson (1952) mentioned only 10 foliicolous species for Argentina, occurring in the provinces of Misiones, Formosa, Jujuy, Tucumán, and Salta. Sérusiaux and De Sloover (1986) gave interesting reports and commentaries about the variation of the foliicolous lichen flora in western Argentina. They included 25 species for the provinces of Misiones, Corrientes, Chaco, and Santiago del Estero. On that occasion, a new species of Gyalectidium, G. eskuchei , was described. My own interest on foliicolous lichens began in 1976, based on collections made in Argentina and adjacent Paraguay and Brazil. Some of the results were already presented or published earlier (Ferraro 1982, 1983). Several genera and species were reported for the first time for the country, and new chorological data added. Up to the present, the following new species were described: Tricharia cuneata Ferraro &amp; Vězda, from Formosa, Fellhanera follmannii Ferraro &amp; Vězda, from Chaco province, and Trichothelium argenteum R. Lucking &amp; Ferraro, from Corrientes. Extensive field work revealed that the occurrence of foliicolous lichens in NE Argentina and adjacent areas is restricted to tropical rain forests. Comparatively high diversity, with 15 families, 25 genera and 80 species, was found in the marginal forest of the Parana river, and is likewise expected along the margins of the Uruguay river and other small rivers of the region. The foliicolous lichen flora of Misiones is more diverse than that of Corrientes, except for the area of Ituzaingó, E of Corrientes, where foliicolous lichens are equally abundant as in Misiones. Actually, the foliicolous flora in NE of Argentina varies greatly along a transect from the East to West. Dominant families are the Asterothyriaceae, Ectolechiaceae, Gomphillaceae, Opegraphaceae, Pilocarpaceae, Strigulaceae, and Trichotheliaceae; whereas the Arthoniaceae, Gyalectaceae, Lecanora- ceae, Microtheliopsidaceae, Phyllobatheliaceae, and Verrucariaceae are less well represented or absent. Foliicolous species of the Thelotremataceae (Chroodiscus) are unknown though corticolous taxa have been found. Apart from typical foliicolous lichens, some observations could be made on other inhabitants of the phyllosphere, on faculatively foliicolous taxa, and on the occasionally corticolous occurrence of typical foliicolous species. Organisms that are frequently found on leaves, besides foliicolous lichens, include epiphyllous bryophytes (Figure 1), non-lichenized algae, such as Phycopeltis and Trentepohlia species (filamentous Cyanophyceae are generally rare), fungi, and insect larvae. Among the fungi, we could identify Parmularia styracis Leveill. (Loculoascomycetes: Microthyriales), growing abundantly on Styrax leprosum (Styracaceae) leaves in Comentes. Some facultatively foliicolous lichens which are quite commonly found on leaf surfaces are Coenogonium spp., Usnea spp., Physcia spp., and representatives of the Parmeliaceae and the Collemaceae, often with very small, immature thalii. Figure 1: Part of a leaf surface with the liverwort Leptolejeunea sp. growing over a thallus of Byssoloma. Some typical foliicolous lichens were also found growing on the cortex of thin twigs or on the leaf petioles, particularly when secondary vegetation is prevailing, e.g. Byssoloma leucoblepharum , Gyalectidium filicinum, Opegrapha filicina , Porina epiphylla , Mazosia rotula , Tricharia sp. (sterile thallus with setae), and Trichothelium sp. They were collected on small branches of Pilocarpus sp. (Rutaceae) in Misiones and Corrientes. It was further observed that members of the Gyalectaceae (Dimer ella epiphylla ) or Pilocarpaceae ( Fellhanera bouteillei ) developed mature thalii between cushes of bryophytes, although without penetrating the epidermis of the bryophytes leaves.
